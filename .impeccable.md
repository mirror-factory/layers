## Design Context

### Users
People who have conversations that matter — sales calls, client meetings, interviews, standups, strategy sessions. They need to capture what was said, understand what matters, and take action on it. They're busy, they context-switch constantly, and they don't want to take notes. The tool should feel like it's doing the work so they can focus on the conversation.

### Brand Personality
**Precise. Effortless. Alive.**

- Precise: every element has a reason. Nothing decorative, nothing wasted.
- Effortless: feels like it anticipates what you need. Low friction, high clarity.
- Alive: responsive, interactive, things move and react. Not static, not stale.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark primary, light mode available. Not "gamer dark" — refined dark. Think Vercel dashboard meets Linear, with more warmth.
- **Accent**: Mint/teal (`oklch(0.75 0.15 175)` range) — the signature color. Used sparingly for active states, key actions, recording indicators.
- **Typography**: Monospace heritage (the app's identity), but paired with a clean proportional font for body readability. Headlines in a distinctive face.
- **Surfaces**: Subtle depth through tinted neutrals, not drop shadows. Cards distinguished by background tone, not borders everywhere.
- **Motion**: Purposeful. Page transitions, recording pulse, status changes. Not bouncy, not flashy — smooth and confident.
- **Anti-references**: Generic SaaS dashboards, Material UI defaults, anything that looks like "a React template." No gradient text, no glassmorphism, no neon-on-dark.
- **References**: Linear (precision), Raycast (speed), Granola (warmth), Vercel (technical confidence)

### Design Principles
1. **Content density over decoration** — show more useful information, less chrome
2. **Interaction over ornamentation** — things should feel alive when you touch them, not just look pretty sitting still
3. **One accent color, used with discipline** — mint means "active, important, actionable." Everything else is neutral.
4. **Progressive disclosure** — simple at first glance, powerful on exploration
5. **Cross-platform coherence** — same design language on web, macOS, and iOS, adapted to each platform's conventions
